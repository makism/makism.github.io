About

The last years I find myself being most productive and decisive to pursue Ph.D. studies. I am currently affiliated with the Cognitive Neuroscience Department, at the Faculty of Neuroscience of Maastricht University, and, at the same time, employed at Brain Innovation B.V. in Maastricht.

My main research focus is brain connectivity and graph theory (and algorithms and applications). The way connectivity varies and changes across brain regions given a cognitive task, mental load, and even neurodegenerative disease. These complex networks can provide meaningful insights, in a magnitude of scales, both temporal and spatial.

At the same time, I am fascinated by real-time big data analytics. Currently, I am in the process of learning and educating myself with notions important to real-time data and business analytics. Making myself familiar with all the appropriate tools of the trade, i.e., Spark, Airflow, Kafka, etc., is a fun bonus!
