About

PUBLISHED ON AUG 17, 2019

If I were to a favorite book, that would be Gibson’s “Neuromancer”; hackers jacking into the cyberspace by projecting their consciousness into a shared virtual space, a hidden layer augmenting the perceived reality. That was cyberpunk at its finest form; humans’ brains interfacing with computers and enhanced cognitive capabilities. Now I am in position to realize the blur between science fiction and reality. Ideas and notions that were previously thought to be science fiction are becoming reality and that is something that overwhelms me.

Quoting Gibson, “My problem is that all things are increasingly interesting to me”, describes precisely my engagement with computer science and neuroscience; studying the brain’s cognition under different tasks, mental loads and even neurodegenerative diseases. More specifically, I am interested in investigating the connectivity between brain regions and studying graph theoretical analyses to explain these established networks. My most recent academic and professional choices are pivoted around these topics.

The last years I find myself being most productive and decisive to pursue PhD studies. I am currently affiliated with the Cognitive Neuroscience Department, at the Faculty of Neuroscience of Maastricht University, and employed at Brain Innovation B.V. in Maastricht.

My main research focus is brain connectivity and graph theory.
